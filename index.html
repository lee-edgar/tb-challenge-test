Overview

Challenge Background

Tuberculosis (TB) remains one of the top infectious disease killers globally, with over 10 million new cases annually. Early and accurate detection through medical imaging is critical — yet access to expert radiologists is severely limited in high-burden regions. TREAT-MMTB 2026 challenges participants to develop AI methods that can bridge this diagnostic gap.
🫁

Task 1: Cavity Detection & Segmentation

Detect and segment lung cavities from chest X-rays — critical markers of TB severity, bacterial load, and patient infectiousness.

🔬

Task 2: Multimodal TB Diagnosis

Combine chest X-rays with clinical data to predict Timika scores, enabling more robust, accurate, and explainable TB diagnosis.

🌍

Multi-Center Validation

External validation across 5 centers in Korea, Mongolia, Peru, and the Philippines, ensuring clinical generalizability and real-world applicability.

Tasks

Challenge Tasks

TREAT-MMTB 2026 features two complementary tasks addressing different aspects of TB assessment. Participants may enter one or both tasks.

Task 1: Cavity Presence Detection and Segmentation

Develop algorithms to detect the presence of lung cavities on chest X-rays and segment them when present.

Clinical Significance

Cavities are critical indicators of TB severity, bacterial load, and infectiousness. Automated detection can accelerate clinical decision-making in resource-limited settings.

Approach

Combined classification (presence/absence) and segmentation pipeline.

Metrics

Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC) Accuracy (Presence Detection)

Evaluation

Evaluation & Submission

Each task uses different metrics appropriate to its clinical objective.
Task 1 — Cavity Segmentation

Metrics

Dice Similarity Coefficient (DSC) Accuracy

DSC evaluates segmentation quality for cavity delineation. Accuracy measures binary cavity presence detection. The final ranking combines both metrics.

Task 2 — Multimodal Diagnosis

Metrics

F1-Score

F1-Score balances precision and recall for TB diagnosis, reflecting clinical requirements where both false positives and false negatives carry significant consequences.

Phase 1 — Internal Validation

Prediction Submission

During the internal validation period, teams submit prediction results for evaluation.

  • Submit prediction files (format specified in GitHub)
  • Results evaluated on internal validation set
  • Leaderboard updated periodically
Phase 2 — External Testing

Docker Submission

Top teams from Phase 1 are invited for external evaluation.

  • Docker image via Docker Hub
  • Model weights included in container
  • README with execution instructions
  • Method Description (MICCAI template, up to 8+2 pages)

Docker specifications and submission guide: GitHub Repository

Guidelines

Rules & Policies

01

Automatic Methods Only

Only fully automatic methods are eligible for final ranking. No manual intervention is allowed during inference.

02

External Data Policy

No additional annotated data beyond the provided dataset is allowed. Publicly available pre-trained models (e.g., ImageNet, pre-trained foundation models) are permitted. All data and model sources must be fully disclosed in the Method Description.

03

Submission Limits

Phase 1 (Internal): Limited submissions per team during the validation period.
Phase 2 (External): Maximum 1 final Docker submission per task.

04

Anti-Cheating & Reproducibility

Top-ranked teams must submit full source code and trained model weights for reproducibility verification. Any form of data leakage, test set probing, or result manipulation will result in immediate disqualification.

05

Team Composition

Each participant may only be a member of one team per task. Team composition changes are permitted until the internal validation phase begins.

06

Data Confidentiality

The dataset is provided exclusively for this challenge. Redistribution, sharing, or any commercial use is strictly prohibited. All participants must agree to the data usage agreement upon registration.

07

Independent Publication

Participating teams are welcome to publish their results independently. Please respect the collective efforts of the challenge and cite the challenge paper when applicable.
