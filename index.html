Overview

Challenge Background

Tuberculosis (TB) remains one of the leading causes of death from infectious diseases worldwide. Early and accurate detection through medical imaging is critical for effective treatment and containment. This challenge invites researchers to develop AI methods for automated TB segmentation, aiming to support clinicians in resource-limited settings where radiologist availability is scarce.
🎯

Task

Segmentation of TB-related lesions from chest imaging. Participants will develop methods to accurately delineate regions of interest for clinical decision support.

🏥

Clinical Impact

Automated TB segmentation can accelerate diagnosis in high-burden regions, reduce inter-observer variability, and enable timely treatment initiation.

Guidelines

Rules & Policies

01

Automatic Methods Only

Only fully automatic segmentation methods are eligible for final ranking. No manual intervention is allowed during inference.

02

No External Data

Use of additional annotated data beyond the provided dataset is prohibited. Publicly available pre-trained models (e.g., ImageNet) are allowed. All data sources must be disclosed.

03

Submission Limits

Phase 1: Up to [N] submissions per team during the leaderboard period.
Phase 2: Maximum 1 final Docker submission.

04

Anti-Cheating Policy

Top-ranked teams must submit source code and trained model weights for reproducibility verification. Any form of data leakage, test set probing, or result manipulation will lead to disqualification.

05

Team Composition

Each participant may only be a member of one team. Teams may have up to [N] members. Team composition changes are allowed until the leaderboard phase begins.

06

Data Usage & Confidentiality

The dataset is provided exclusively for this challenge. Redistribution, sharing, or commercial use is strictly prohibited. All participants must agree to the data usage agreement upon registration.
